The Origin of Bubble Tea





Bubble Tea is a pretty popular and common beverage in Taiwan. It is also a must-try beverage when you visit Taiwan. Bubble tea originated from 1980â€™s. There are two stores/companies claim that they are the first bubble tea creator. One store is Chun Shui Tang and the other store is Hanlin Tea Room.

Chun Shui Tang starts their business from a simple tea shop store in Taichung, Taiwan in 1983. The owner, Mr. Han-Jie Liu setup a tea store to produce tea beverage and introduce traditional tea culture. In the early 1980s, he experimented with cold milk tea by adding fruit, syrup, candied yams, and tapioca bubbles. Then, the bubble tea become a popular beverage everywhere in Taiwan.

The other store is Hanlin Tea Room originated in a market in Tainan, Taiwan in 1987. Bubble originally is white color and change to black color, which is the bubble now we see. Both stores did not apply patent for bubble tea. There is still a mystery of who is the first creator.

During the 1990s, the drink became popular in most parts of East and Southeast Asia. Since that time, it has spread to regions around the world, including the U.S., Australia, Europe, and most recently South Africa.
